I am an AI Research Scientist at the Samsung AI Center in Warsaw, Poland, where I work on Safety & Alignment of large language models.

I recently earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Sapienza University of Rome, where I was a member of the Perception and Intelligence Lab led by Prof. Fabio Galasso. During my doctoral studies, I dedicated a year to an internship as an AI Researcher at Panasonic North America, in Mountain View. Concurrently, I held a visiting researcher position at UC Berkeley within Trevor Darrell's lab at BAIR.

The focus of my past research has been on computer vision and trustworthy AI, with interests spanning self-supervised learning, multimodal systems, active learning, and hyperbolic neural networks. I have hands-on experience in action recognition, domain adaptation, and semantic segmentation, often under low-label or cross-domain scenarios.

I hold a Master's degree in Data Science from Sapienza University of Rome, where I graduated in 2021 (110/110 cum laude). Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Roma Tre University in 2019. During my undergraduate studies, I interned at Claranet IT, where I developed a serverless application for monitoring event-driven architectures on AWS.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, photography, running, playing the guitar, and working out at the gym. Feel free to check out some of my photos on Unsplash.
